How to Choose the Right Commercial Kitchen Cleaning Service

Home / Blog / Choosing a Service
Tips Business Guide

Selecting the right commercial kitchen exhaust cleaning service is crucial for maintaining compliance, ensuring safety, and protecting your investment. With so many options available, how do you choose the best provider for your facility?

1. Verify Certifications and Qualifications

The first and most important factor is ensuring the company has proper certifications:

  • NFPA 96 Certification: Technicians should be certified in NFPA 96 standards
  • Insurance Coverage: Verify they carry liability and workers' compensation insurance
  • Business License: Ensure they're properly licensed in your area
  • Industry Memberships: Look for membership in professional organizations like IKECA (International Kitchen Exhaust Cleaning Association)

Pro Tip: Don't just take their word for itâ€”ask to see copies of certifications and insurance certificates.

2. Experience and Track Record

Experience matters in commercial kitchen cleaning. Consider:

  • How long has the company been in business?
  • Do they have experience with facilities similar to yours?
  • Can they provide references from current clients?
  • What is their reputation in the industry?
  • Do they have experience with restaurant chains or multi-location facilities?

3. Comprehensive Service Offerings

A quality cleaning service should offer more than just basic hood cleaning:

  • Complete exhaust system cleaning (hood, ductwork, fans)
  • Filter cleaning and replacement services
  • Fire suppression system cleaning
  • Detailed documentation and reporting
  • Before and after photos
  • Compliance tracking and scheduling
  • Emergency services availability

4. Cleaning Methods and Equipment

Ask about their cleaning process and equipment:

  • Do they clean to bare metal as required by NFPA 96?
  • What cleaning agents and methods do they use?
  • Do they have specialized equipment for hard-to-reach areas?
  • How do they protect your kitchen during cleaning?
  • What is their process for waste disposal?

5. Documentation and Reporting

Proper documentation is essential for compliance and insurance purposes. Look for:

  • Detailed service reports after each cleaning
  • Before and after photographs
  • Compliance stickers with cleaning dates
  • Digital access to service history
  • Automated scheduling and reminder systems
  • Reports that meet insurance and inspection requirements

6. Pricing and Value

While cost is important, it shouldn't be the only factor:

What to Look For Red Flags
Transparent, itemized pricing Prices significantly lower than competitors
Written quotes and contracts Verbal agreements only
Value-added services included Hidden fees and surcharges
Flexible payment options Cash-only operations

Remember: The cheapest option often ends up costing more in the long run if the work isn't done properly.

7. Scheduling Flexibility

Your cleaning service should work around your business hours:

  • Do they offer after-hours or weekend service?
  • Can they accommodate emergency cleanings?
  • How far in advance do you need to schedule?
  • Do they provide automated scheduling and reminders?
  • What is their cancellation and rescheduling policy?

8. Customer Service and Communication

Good communication is essential for a successful partnership:

  • How responsive are they to inquiries?
  • Do they have a dedicated account manager?
  • Can you easily reach them in case of emergencies?
  • Do they provide proactive compliance reminders?
  • How do they handle complaints or issues?

Questions to Ask Potential Providers

  1. Are your technicians NFPA 96 certified?
  2. Can you provide proof of insurance?
  3. What is included in your standard cleaning service?
  4. How do you determine cleaning frequency for my facility?
  5. What documentation do you provide after each service?
  6. Do you offer emergency services?
  7. Can you provide references from similar facilities?
  8. What is your process if we fail an inspection?
  9. Do you offer multi-location management for restaurant chains?
  10. What sets you apart from your competitors?

Red Flags to Watch Out For

Be cautious if a company exhibits any of these warning signs:

  • Unable or unwilling to provide proof of certifications or insurance
  • Prices that seem too good to be true
  • No written contracts or service agreements
  • Poor online reviews or no verifiable references
  • Pressure tactics or high-pressure sales
  • Lack of professional documentation or reporting
  • No emergency contact information
  • Unwillingness to answer questions about their process
