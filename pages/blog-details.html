Complete Guide to NFPA 96 Compliance

Home / Blog / NFPA 96 Guide
Compliance NFPA 96 Safety

NFPA 96 is the standard for ventilation control and fire protection of commercial cooking operations. Understanding and implementing these requirements is essential for every restaurant owner and facility manager.

What is NFPA 96?

NFPA 96, titled "Ventilation Control and Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations," provides the minimum safety requirements for the design, installation, operation, inspection, and maintenance of ventilation control and fire protection of commercial cooking operations.

Key Requirements

  • Commercial cooking equipment must have an exhaust system that removes smoke, grease, and odors
  • Grease filters must be listed for the intended purpose and installed properly
  • Exhaust systems must be cleaned to bare metal at intervals based on cooking volume
  • Fire suppression systems must be installed and maintained according to NFPA 10
  • Cooking equipment must be protected by a listed fire suppression system

Cleaning Frequency Guidelines

The cleaning frequency depends on the volume of cooking:

Cooking VolumeCleaning Frequency
High (24/7 operation)Monthly
Medium ( breakfast, lunch, dinner)Quarterly
Low (limited cooking)Semi-annually

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with NFPA 96 can result in:

  • Fire hazards and increased risk of kitchen fires
  • Failed health inspections
  • fines and penalties from local authorities
  • Insurance coverage issues or cancellations
  • Potential liability in case of fire or injury
