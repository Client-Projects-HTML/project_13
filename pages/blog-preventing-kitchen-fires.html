Safety Prevention

Commercial kitchen fires are a major risk for restaurants. The combination of high heat, open flames, and grease buildup creates a dangerous environment. However, most kitchen fires are preventable with proper maintenance and cleaning practices.

The Role of Grease

Grease is the fuel for most kitchen fires. As cooking vapors rise, grease particles settle on the hood, filters, and inside ductwork. If not cleaned regularly, this buildup can ignite from a flare-up on the cooktop, sending fire rapidly spreading through the exhaust system to the roof.

Top Prevention Tips

  • Regular Professional Cleaning: ensure your exhaust system is cleaned to bare metal by certified professionals.
  • Daily Grease Filter Maintenance: Clean filters daily to prevent obstruction and buildup.
  • Inspect Grease Traps: Keep grease traps clean to prevent overflow and hazardous backup.
  • Employee Training: Train staff on how to use fire suppression systems and Class K extinguishers.
